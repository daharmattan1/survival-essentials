## Recommended Books (Choose 1-2 Max)

### SAS Survival Handbook by John "Lofty" Wiseman

**Why**: Gold standard comprehensive reference
**Strengths**: Covers everything, illustrated, field-tested
**Format**: Physical book recommended (no power needed)
**Use case**: Keep one copy at home, one in car

### Build the Perfect Survival Kit by John McCann

**Why**: Focuses on gear selection and preparation
**Strengths**: Practical, realistic scenarios
**Use case**: Building bug-out bag or home kit

### Deep Survival by Laurence Gonzales

**Why**: Psychology of survival, why people live or die
**Strengths**: Mindset and decision-making under stress
**Use case**: Understanding the mental game

## Hands-On Training (High Value)

### Red Cross First Aid/CPR Course

**Why**: Medical skills are most likely to be needed
**Format**: In-person, 4-8 hours
**Certification**: 2 years
**Cost**: ~$100
**Action**: Take once, recertify every 2 years

### Local Community Emergency Response Team (CERT)

**Why**: Free government-sponsored disaster training
**Format**: 20 hours over 7-8 weeks
**Content**: Fire safety, medical, search/rescue, disaster psychology
**Cost**: Free
**Action**: Search "[your city] CERT training"

### Basic Wilderness Survival Course

**Why**: Hands-on fire, shelter, navigation practice
**Format**: Weekend workshop (many outdoor schools offer)
**Cost**: $200-500
**Action**: Optional, but beats learning by trial-and-error

## Gear (Minimal, High-Impact)

### Tier 1: Must Have

1. **Water purification** - LifeStraw, Sawyer Mini, or tablets
2. **Fire starting** - 2 lighters + waterproof matches
3. **Flashlight/headlamp** - LED, extra batteries
4. **First aid kit** - Pre-made or DIY
5. **Multi-tool** - Leatherman or similar
6. **Emergency blanket** - Mylar space blanket

**Cost**: ~$100-150 total

### Tier 2: Nice to Have

1. **Portable radio** - Hand-crank or battery
2. **Water storage** - 5-gallon jugs or WaterBricks
3. **Camp stove** - Propane/butane for cooking
4. **Emergency food** - 72-hour supply (Mountain House, etc.)
5. **Tarp** - 10Ã—10 ft for shelter or water collection

**Cost**: ~$200-300 total

### Tier 3: Advanced

1. **Generator** - Propane or dual-fuel
2. **Ham radio** - Baofeng UV-5R (entry-level)
3. **Solar charger** - For phones/batteries
4. **Firearm** (if trained and comfortable)

**Cost**: $500-2000+

## Regular Practice (Use It or Lose It)

### Quarterly Drills

- **Fire drill**: Practice escape routes, meeting points
- **Comms check**: Test radios, update contact lists
- **Gear check**: Replace expired food, batteries, medications

### Annual Activities

- **Power outage simulation**: Live 24 hours without electricity
- **First aid review**: Re-read procedures, check kit
- **Route planning**: Update evacuation routes, rally points

### Skills to Practice

1. **Fire starting** - Do it 10 times until it's easy
2. **Water purification** - Test your methods at home
3. **Shelter building** - Camp in backyard, test tarp setups
4. **First aid** - Practice bandaging, splinting on family

**Why**: Stress + unfamiliarity = failure. Practice = muscle memory.

## What NOT to Buy

### Overrated/Unnecessary

1. **Tactical gear obsession** - You're not special forces
2. **50+ types of survival knives** - One good knife > 10 cheap ones
3. **Freeze-dried food for years** - Start with 2 weeks, not 2 years
4. **Expensive bug-out bags** - Build your own, know every item
5. **Paracord bracelets** - Just carry 50 ft of paracord
6. **Zombie survival books** - Focus on realistic scenarios

### Focus Instead On

- Skills over gear
- Realistic scenarios over fantasies
- Redundancy over single expensive items
- Practice over collection
